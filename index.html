Hussein of Jordan

The man who built a country that has no resources

King Hussein (1935 - 1999) gives a press conference in Amman, Jordan, 25th August 1958. Crown Prince Muhammad can be seen behind, second from left wearing a black beret.

Here's a time line of King Hussein life:

  • 1935 - Born in Amman, Transjordan (now Jordan).
  • 1952 - King Talal abdicated due to illness and King Hussein became king at the age of 16.
  • 1953 - King Hussein was formally crowned king of Jordan.
  • 1957 - King Hussein’s first son, Abdullah II, was born.
  • 1962 - King Hussein established the University of Jordan.
  • 1976 - King Hussein established the King Hussein Medical Center (KHMC) which is a military medical complex that has five hospitals situated in Amman, Jordan.
  • 1980 - King Hussein established the Royal Scientific Society (RSS) which is an independent non-profit organization that aims to advance scientific research and development in Jordan.
  • 1981 - he established the King Hussein Cancer Center (KHCC) which is a non-profit comprehensive cancer center that provides state-of-the-art cancer care and treatment to patients in Jordan and throughout the Middle East.
  • 1985 - King Hussein established the Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST) which is a public university located in Irbid, Jordan.
  • 1993 - King Hussein established the Princess Haya Biotechnology Center (PHBC) which is a research center that focuses on biotechnology research and development.
  • 1995 - he established the King Hussein Foundation (KHF) which is an independent, non-governmental organization that seeks to build upon King Hussein’s humanitarian vision and legacy by promoting peace, security and development in Jordan and throughout the Middle East.
  • 1999 - King Hussein died in Amman at the age of 63.
